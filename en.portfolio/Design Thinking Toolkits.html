Design Thinking Toolkits

Design thinking template covered all design phases

Project Introduction

User research always should be the first step to dig deeper about user problems before start the UX design. But in different design phase, which the research method I should select? how to conduct the user research? what are the points I need to pay attention? Do I need to created the research material every time? All of these questions are around me after I have done the same research process for several times. Finally, Yiting, another designer in Sea, and I proposed and lead the design thinking toolkit template project to provide reusable different design thinking kits in all design phase to improve the design team efficiency.

Design Thinking Toolkit Overview

What is Design Thiking Toolkits?

Design Thinking Kit is a all-in-one resource center aim to make the design process standard and efficient for UXers to apply design techniques and research methods to all kinds of projects. This toolkit provides us with:

  • Handy research templates that can be quickly apply to different stages of the UX design process.
  • Unified methods, layouts and outputs makes our design deliverables more consistent and standard across the team.
  • One source of truth. All UX templates can live in one place for the team to refer from.
  • Team collaboration. All of us can be the contributors to constantly enhance or add new templates in to make it better for our actual usage.

* Disclamier: When it comes to user research, there is no ‚Äòone size fits all‚Äô approach. It all depends on the specific user group and context you are solving for. The research templates are just the bases for you to quickly get started, you should modify each template to tailor for your actual use cases. It is your job as the designer to define and plan the research for every project.

When to use what template?

If you are still in clueless in what template to use, here is a guide that may help you select the template for your project use at different design stage.

How to arrange the templates in my project?

When you decided which template you want to use, simply copy from here or look for them in the [Shared] Toolkit library, drag and drop the template to the designated area. Start working on it to fit your project context.

For comprehensive and in-depth research that contains multiple parts such as competitor analysis, focus group study, user survey, etc, are included in the [Template][B] UX Project Template. Modify them for your project, or remove if no use.

For the light-weighted templates that are important to developers, we recommend place them at the first layer, under [Annex] section. See example below.

Workshop and Sharing

Workshop

Conduct workshop to allow users to know more about how to use the template in real project:

  • Pick up the suitable template to conduct workshop.
  • Prepare the topic about the workshop.
  • Prepare the process about how to run the workshop.
  • Invite the designer to participate in the workshop.
  • Collect feedback about how to conduct the workshop next time.

Sharing

Encourage and organize sharing when designer share their experience when using template in real project.
